# Concepts

A Domain Story document captures one narrative flow through a domain. The structure follows the original Domain Storytelling canon and exists at three levels: the story itself, the Sentences inside it, and the elements – Actors, Work Objects, edges – inside the Sentences.

This chapter walks through each concept on its own page:

- An **[Actor](/extensions/domain-storytelling/concepts/actor.md)** is someone or something that does things in the domain.
- A **[Work Object](/extensions/domain-storytelling/concepts/work-object.md)** is something that gets produced, consumed, transferred, or transformed.
- A **[Sentence](/extensions/domain-storytelling/concepts/sentence.md)** is one numbered unit of the story, drawn as a set of arrows between Actors and Work Objects.
- A **[Group](/extensions/domain-storytelling/concepts/group.md)** is an arbitrary cluster of elements the story wants to mark together.
